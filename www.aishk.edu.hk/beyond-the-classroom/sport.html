Sport

AISHK sees sport as key in providing a balanced education, fostering student wellbeing and enhancing physical skills.

Our sports program encourages all students to “give it a go” regardless of skill level, to pursue enjoyment of sport and to strive for their personal best. Along with this approach, we ensure opportunities for students who excel in sport to compete in a wide range of tournaments, representing AISHK locally and internationally.

The AISHK sports brand is the Kangaroos - an icon of Australian wildlife. Emulating strength, speed, power and resilience, the emblem is worn proudly by AISHK students and staff, symbolising our commitment to teamwork and sporting excellence.

Representative Sport

In the wider community, AISHK embraces competitive sport, representing the school through our strong affiliation with a number of local and international sporting clubs and associations. 

An avenue to build rapport and develop lasting friendships, representative sport at AISHK also demonstrates to students the importance of developing life skills such as teamwork and leadership. See our wide range of sports news and fixtures coverage here.

International Schools Sports Federation Hong Kong (ISSFHK)

The ISSFHK is the main domestic sports association that AISHK is affiliated with, for secondary school students. With over 35 member schools, AISHK competes in over 12 different sports codes in many leagues, tournaments, meets and competitions.

ISSFHK Tournaments:

  • Season 1: U14/16/20 Volleyball, U14 Soccer, U16/20 Netball, U12/14/16/20 Swimming 
  • Season 2: U12/14/16/20 Basketball, U16 Soccer, Golf, U12/14/16/20 Cross Country 
  • Season 3: U12/14/16/20 Badminton, U20 Soccer, U12/14/16/20 Athletics 
  • Season 4: U12/14 Netball, U14/20 Touch, U12 Soccer, U12 Volleyball, U14/20 Cricket


Hong Kong Primary Schools Sports Association (HKPSSA)

The HKPSSA includes 20 of the top international primary schools in Hong Kong as member schools. This organisation focuses on ensuring as many young athletes as possible are exposed to interschool competitions. Promoting the values of sport and ensuring students enjoy positive experiences, the format of these events are typically single-day tournaments.  

HKPSSA Tournaments

  • Season 1: Netball, Soccer, AFL Auskick
  • Season 2: Basketball, Tag Rugby Cross-country
  • Season 3: Cricket, Aquathon, Swimming, Touch Football

Southern Delta Region Conference (SDRC)

The SDRC is a small and unique group of top schools from the Greater Bay Area including two schools from Guangzhou, two from Shenzhen and two from Hong Kong. Focused on four core sports, it is aimed at ‘middle school’ age students (Under 14). Tournaments are run over 2-3 days and include international travel (via land) and hotel stays. 

SDRC Sports:

  • Touch Football 
  • Volleyball 
  • Basketball

Australian International Schools Association (AISA)

The AISA conference of schools convenes once a year with the common underpinning aspect that its member schools offer an Australian and international education. With 10 member schools from all over Asia and as far afield as the Middle East, AISA includes schools from Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, UAE, Vietnam, Cambodia and Hong Kong. Over three days, the schools compete at Under 16 level in five different sporting codes.

AISA Sports:

  • Soccer 
  • Touch Football
  • Basketball
  • Netball
  • Swimming

Association of China and Mongolia International Schools (ACAMIS)

The ACAMIS organisation has over 80 member schools from throughout the China regions. AISHK is an aligned school, meaning that we will compete in core sports with a representative division. Our division is Gold and includes schools from Shenzhen, Xiamen, Shanghai, Beijing and Dalian. These core sports events include international travel and hotel stays.

ACAMIS Sports:

  • Volleyball 
  • Basketball 
  • Soccer

Hong Kong school Sports Federation (HKSSF)

The HKSSF is the main, government-run and sanctioned schools sport federation. AISHK competes in a variety of sports with significant involvement swimming.

HKSSF Sports:

  • Season 1: Netball, Soccer, AFL Auskick 
  • Season 2: Basketball, Tag Rugby Cross-country
  • Season 3: Cricket, Aquathon, Swimming, Touch Footbal

Personal Development, Health and Physical Education (PDHPE)

Within the school curriculum, our Personal Development, Health and Physical Education (PDHPE) program caters to students from Early Childhood through to Year 12. The engaging syllabus promotes not only physical fitness, but a healthy approach to mindfully attaining a well-rounded lifestyle and aligns with facets of our sports programme. Our students learn important life skills that will serve them well both on and off the field.

Interhouse Sport

AISHK adopts a House system which showcases the vibrant community spirit, healthy competition and inclusive values that define our school. The House system fosters a sense of teamwork and belonging for students across across all year levels in sport, the arts, student leadership and community service. The four AISHK Houses, named after Australian flora, are Eucalypt, Jacaranda, Waratah, and Wattle.

The interhouse sports program extends beyond the pursuit of victory, ensuring that every student, regardless of their level of skill or ability, feels valued and supported. Whether a student is an aspiring athlete or simply enjoys participation, they will find their place in a nurturing environment across a wide range of interhouse sports, including basketball, volleyball, soccer, swimming and athletics. Interhouse events see staff and students cheer passionately for their teammates, displaying true camaraderie amongst all participants.
